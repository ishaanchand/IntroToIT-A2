Intro to IT: Team Unsure - Assignment 2

Interview with an IT professional

For the IT Work section of this assignment, our group decided to interview an IT professional. The professional we interviewed is Rajiv Lal, who is currently a Client Partner at Wipro, which is an Indian multinational corporation that provides information technology, consulting, and business process services.

Q: Rajiv, tell us about the industry you work in

A: I work in the telecommunications industry.

Q: Please tell us about your IT work, what exactly do you do?

A: I am responsible for business of a $25M portfolio. Primarily we sell IT professional services cutting across the entire value chain of Telecom services. We mainly sell our services as System Integrators for various commercial off the shelf (COTS) products like Salesforce, CloudSense, ServiceNow etc. Also, we do lot of work with software required for Digital Transformations like React, NodeJs, Java, Springboot etc.

Q: Have there been any noticeable trends in the types of products offered in the last 5 years?

A: There is lot of demand for off the shelf (COTS) products which have been built on industry best practices and can be implemented quickly with minimum customisation. Cloud Native implementations are also in big demand.

Q: What products do you think will be the most sought after in the next 5 years?

A: Open-source technologies will be in great demand in near future, with more and more things moving to cloud, cyber security will be in focus. Data plays a key role in any implementation, with more companies embarking on Digitization journey, data technologies and professional will be in demand not to mention the importance of Analytics. Automation will also play a key role and technologies involving RPA, Blockchain, AI, ML, DevOps, DevSecOps will gain lot of momentum in coming days.

Q: What other kinds of work do you have to do?

A: My work is predominantly related to the sales sector, finding new business opportunities in a similar space, and growing our existing business.

Q: Who are the different people at work you interact with?

A: In terms of my people interaction, it is mostly external which are basically the customers of the business and regarding internal conversation my organisation team.

Q: Please tell us about interactions with other IT professionals

A: I deal with various levels of IT professionals. From customer side its mainly with the Executives, GM’s, and engineering leads. Within my organisation I interact with software developers, testers, business analysts and project managers.

Q: What about your conversations with clients and investors of the firm?

A: With client and investors, discussions are primarily around trying to find a solution for a business or IT problem they are grappling with. Also, we look at avenues to add value in terms of enabling our customers achieve their KPI’s, be it increase in sales or improving customer satisfaction/experience.

Q: What aspects of your work do you spend most of your time on?

A: Most of my time is spent on identifying white spaces within the customer business where I can sell my company services, help customer solve their needs and ensure to sustain the running business.

Q: Can you share an example of your work which best captures the essence of the IT industry?

A: I have a 120-member team managing the portal for all the enterprise customers for one of the largest Telco in Australia. We use all the latest cutting-edge technologies in terms of software and infrastructure.
